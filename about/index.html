Our Story

When I lost my grandmother in 2019, I spent weeks gathering photos, recipes, and stories from family members. Her famous apple pie recipe was on a faded index card. Her voice was captured in a single voicemail I couldn't bear to delete. Cousins across the country had photos I'd never seen.

I realized how fragile these memories were‚Äîscattered across devices, fading in boxes, living only in the minds of those who knew her. And I thought: there has to be a better way.

Forever Fields is that better way. It's the place I wish existed when I was grieving. A beautiful, permanent home for every photo, every story, every recipe that made her who she was. A place where our whole family could contribute and visit, no matter where they lived.

We built Forever Fields not as a business, but as a labor of love. Every feature exists because we've been where you are. We know the weight of loss, and we know the comfort of preserving what remains.
